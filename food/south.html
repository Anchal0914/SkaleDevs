idli

Idli is a South Indian staple. These steamed rice and urad dal (black gram) cakes are light, fluffy, and spongy. Often served with coconut chutney and sambar, idli is a healthy and versatile breakfast or snack item enjoyed across India.

Rasam

Rasam is a South Indian soup-like dish. It's made from tamarind juice, tomatoes, and a blend of spices like black pepper, cumin, and coriander. Often served with rice, it's known for its tangy, spicy, and aromatic flavors, and is believed to have digestive benefits.

pongal

Pongal is a traditional South Indian dish made during the harvest festival of the same name. It's a creamy rice and lentil porridge cooked with black pepper, cumin, ginger, and ghee. Often garnished with cashews and curry leaves, Pongal is a flavorful and wholesome meal..

Uttapam

Uttapam is a South Indian pancake made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram) batter. It's thicker than dosa and typically includes toppings like onions, tomatoes, and green chilies. Crispy on the outside and soft inside, uttapam is served with chutney and sambar..

Bhelpuri

Bhel Puri is a popular Indian street food. It's a savory snack made from puffed rice mixed with chopped vegetables like tomatoes, onions, and cilantro, seasoned with tangy tamarind chutney and spices. This crunchy and flavorful dish is a beloved snack across India.

Upma

Upma is a South Indian breakfast dish made from semolina (rava) cooked with spices, vegetables, and sometimes nuts. It's seasoned with mustard seeds and curry leaves, resulting in a savory, grainy porridge-like texture. Upma is often served with chutney or pickle.

Appam

Appam is a South Indian pancake made from fermented rice batter. It has a soft, fluffy center and a crispy, lacy edge. Traditionally prepared in a special curved pan, appam is a popular breakfast or snack, often served with coconut milk or a savory side dish.

Medu vada

Medu Vada is a South Indian fritter made from urad dal (black gram) batter seasoned with spices, curry leaves, and sometimes chopped onions. These deep-fried, crispy, and doughnut-shaped snacks are a popular South Indian breakfast or snack, often served with coconut chutney and sambar.
