Litti Chokha is a traditional dish from the Indian state of Bihar. It consists of round wheat flour balls (litti) stuffed with roasted gram flour and spices, served with chokha, a mixture of mashed vegetables like eggplant, tomatoes, and spices. It's a flavorful and rustic meal.

Misal pav

Machher Jhol is a Bengali fish curry. It features fish pieces cooked in a spicy and tangy gravy with mustard oil, turmeric, and various spices. This traditional dish is a staple of Bengali cuisine and is often served with rice.

Modak

Rasgulla is a popular Indian dessert. It consists of spongy, soft cheese balls soaked in a light sugar syrup flavored with cardamom. Originating from West Bengal, it's a sweet treat enjoyed across India, especially during festivals and celebrations..

Dhokla

Dalma is a traditional dish from the Indian state of Odisha. It consists of lentils (usually toor dal) cooked with vegetables like pumpkin and drumsticks, flavored with mustard seeds, and tempered with spices like turmeric and ginger. This wholesome and nutritious dish is often served with rice.

Bhelpuri

Momos are a popular Tibetan and Nepali snack. They are dumplings typically filled with minced meat or vegetables, seasoned with garlic, ginger, and spices. Steamed or fried, they are served with dipping sauces and are enjoyed worldwide for their delicious taste and versatility.

Misel

Thukpa is a Tibetan noodle soup, also popular in neighboring regions like Nepal and Bhutan. It features wheat or rice noodles in a flavorful broth with vegetables and often includes meat or tofu. Thukpa is seasoned with various spices and herbs, making it a comforting and hearty dish.

Pab Bhaji

Rasmalai is a delightful Indian dessert. It consists of soft, spongy cheese patties soaked in sweet, saffron-infused milk, garnished with pistachios or almonds. This creamy and aromatic dessert is a favorite at celebrations and provides a rich and indulgent treat.
